The maps & HUD release: open League's classic pre-mapgeo maps, edit HUD layouts visually, and preview your skins standing in a real map — with Twisted Treeline joining the asset packs.

## 🗺 Open legacy (NVR) maps (M141)
- **Open Legacy Map** loads a classic `LEVELS/MapN` folder — the pre-mapgeo `Scene/room.nvr` format — as a standalone, orbitable scene: full geometry, textures and the map's own `Light.dat`.
- **Twisted Treeline (Map10)** joins Crystal Scar as a downloadable asset pack (~73 MB).

## 🌄 Twisted Treeline's ground, rendered the way the game does (M142)
Map10 doesn't blend its ground like other maps — its four tile layers are **height**-blended (by tile alpha), not blended through an authored RGB mask, and the game bakes the result into a single ground atlas. Reproducing that took getting several things right:
- The atlas (`compositeColorMap`) is a **planar world-space bake** over the level's footprint, not something the mesh's second UV set indexes — that UV is the four-blend mask UV and maps somewhere else entirely. The ground now samples it by world XZ over the `__LevelSize_` rect.
- On top of that, the four tile layers **height-blend at full detail** using the painted per-layer scales in `terrainHeightScale`, with the baked atlas modulating them like a lightmap — so you get crisp tile detail *and* the game's baked lighting, instead of a blurry photo of the ground.
- **Structures, trees and props** are lit by the night lighting League bakes into their vertex colours (a dark, blue-tinted ambient plus baked shadowing), so they match the ground's mood instead of sitting under a flat neutral light.
- Meshes League lightmapped with a *separate* texture ship with black vertex colours — stairs, rubble, pebble steps and ground decals. They no longer render as black holes.
- **Ground decals** (mud paths, tower and step decals) paint **once** with smooth alpha-blended edges, instead of tiling into hard seams.

## 🖥 HUD editor (M140)
- A visual viewer + inspector for League's HUD: the UI element tree, each element's rect and its cropped slice of the texture atlas, laid out in the game's reference resolution.
- **Wheel-zoom, drag-pan and a 16:9 guide**, so elements parked far off-canvas no longer push everything out of view.
- Large HUDs composite once into a cached layer rather than redrawing hundreds of images every frame — the big ones are smooth now.

## 🎛 Choose which map previews behind your skins (M142.8)
- Settings ▸ Preview now lists the **map packs** — Crystal Scar and Twisted Treeline — each downloading on demand and selectable as the backdrop, with a badge marking the active one. It replaces the old hardcoded single-map button.

## 🔧 Fixes
- Opening a legacy map after previewing a skin no longer renders **both maps at once** (the character-preview backdrop persists between previews by design, but a legacy map is itself the subject, so it now clears it).
- The HUD editor **crashed on open**; the viewport also gained a crash log at `%AppData%/ReyEngine/crash.log` so a crash leaves a readable stack behind.
- Dependency updates, including an **Avalonia security fix**.
